Title: BA in Education, Communities and Organizations

[Page 1] BA in Education, Communities and Organizations
[Page 1] The Bachelor of Arts in Education, Communities and Organizations (ECO) provides students with a solid foundation in learning theory, human
[Page 1] development, equity studies, organizational theory and community-based research and practice. Through elective courses, students will explore
[Page 1] diverse areas of study. All students complete a community-based capstone.
[Page 1] GENERAL EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS
[Page 1] English Composition (5 credits)
[Page 1] A&H ( 15 credits - Arts and Humanities )
[Page 1] Writing (10 credits) * 5 credits met w/ EDUC 251
[Page 1] Ssc ( 15 credits - Social Sciences )
[Page 1] Diversity (5 credits) * met w/ EDUC 251
[Page 1] NSc ( 15 credits - Natural Sciences )
[Page 1] QSR ( 4-5 credits-Quantitative & Symbolic Reasoning)
[Page 1] Additional 15 credits for Areas of Knowledge-A&H/Ssc/NSc
[Page 1] Additional requirements-minimum 2.0 GPA cumulative/minimum of 180 total credits/45 of last 60 credits taken at UW *Final Year Residency Requirement

[Page 1] EDUC 280 Introduction to Education, Communities and Organizations : must earn a 2.0 or higher A/W/Sp 3
[Page 1] English Composition: must earn a 2.5 or higher
[Page 1] 45 credits: [ 45 or more credits must be completed before quarter of admission]
[Page 1] GPA: Cumulative GPA must be 2.5 or higher
[Page 1] ECO MAJOR REQUIREMENTS
[Page 1] EDUCATION, COMMUNITIES AND ORGANIZATIONS CORE COURSES (26 credits)
[Page 1] EDUC 251 Seeking Educational Equity and Diversity SSc | DIV A/W/Sp 5
[Page 1] EDUC 370 Learning Within and Across Settings A/W 5
[Page 1] EDUC 460 ECO Capstone Internship Preparation (requires enrollment in both quarters) W/Sp 0.5
[Page 1] EDUC 472 Individuals and Organizations SSc A/W 5
[Page 1] EDUC 473 Community-based Research and Practice (prereq: EDUC 251) W/Sp 5
[Page 1] ONE course in Human Development from the following options:
[Page 1] EDPSY 302 Child Development and Learning Ssc A/W/Sp 5
[Page 1] EDPSY 404 Adolescent Development A/W 5
[Page 1] EDPSY 380 Adult Learning and Development Ssc W/Sp 5
[Page 1] EDUCATION, COMMUNITIES AND ORGANIZATIONS ELECTIVE COURSE CHOICES (25 credits)
[Page 1] The electives in the ECO major are meant to reflect the interest areas of the student. Many College of Education course may count toward elective
[Page 1] credits from the following areas of study: Early Childhood & Family Studies (ECFS), Education (EDUC), Educational Curriculum & Instruction (EDC&I),
[Page 1] Educational Psychology (EDPSY), Special Education (EDSPE), Educational Leadership & Policy Studies (EDLPS). See DARS for approved electives.
