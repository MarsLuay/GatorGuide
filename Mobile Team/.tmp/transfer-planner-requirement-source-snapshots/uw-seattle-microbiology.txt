[Page 1] MICROBIOLOGY
[Page 1] Bachelor of Science
[Page 1] Microbiology is the study of the smallest living organisms (bacteria, algae, fungi, and protozoa) and viruses.
[Page 1] Because of their simplicity and rapid growth rates, microorganisms are readily amenable to study in the laboratory.
[Page 1] Because their cellular processes closely resemble those of higher organisms, they are ideally suited for
[Page 1] fundamental studies of biological structure, physiology, metabolism, genetics and development. The
[Page 1] Microbiology department at the University of Washington works mainly in cutting edge research in Bacteriology
[Page 1] and Virology.
[Page 1] The B.S. in Microbiology is complemented by the College of Arts and Sciences general educational requirements
[Page 1] such as English Composition, Writing, Foreign Language, Quantitative & Symbolic Reasoning, Arts
[Page 1] &Humanities, Social Science and Natural Science courses which provide a comprehensive liberal arts degree.
[Page 1] Most students are admitted to the major at the beginning of their junior year. For Admission, students
[Page 1] must complete the following requirements:
[Page 1] 1. A minimum of 75 credits applicable to graduation
[Page 1] 2. A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 overall
[Page 1] 3. A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 in prerequisite CHEM and BIOL courses listed in items 4-6 below.
[Page 1] 4. Completion of BIOL 180, BIOL 200 (minimum grade 2.3), AND BIOL 220.
[Page 1] 5. Completion of one of the following chemistry series:
[Page 1] a. CHEM 142, CHEM 152, CHEM 162
[Page 1] b. CHEM 143, CHEM 153
[Page 1] c. CHEM 145, CHEM 155, CHEM 165
[Page 1] 6. Completion of either CHEM 223, CHEM 237, or CHEM 335
[Page 1] All students are strongly encouraged to meet with the Microbiology Adviser before declaring and at least
[Page 1] quarterly after acceptance to go over academic progress, academic goals, and department policies.

[Page 1] Grade Requirements:
[Page 1] 1. All courses taken to fulfill requirements must be taken for a letter (numerical) grade unless the course is
[Page 1] offered credit/no credit.
[Page 1] 2. Students must maintain a cumulative GPA of 2.25 in all classes that count towards the Microbiology
[Page 1] Major.
[Page 1] 3. A minimum grade of 1.8 must be achieved in all required and elective courses (see below) for them to
[Page 1] be used toward degree requirements for graduation.
[Page 1] Progress/Dismissal Policy:
[Page 1] A student is placed on departmental academic probation for one quarter, if the student's cumulative GPA in
[Page 1] classes that count towards the Microbiology Major falls below 2.25. If at the end of the quarter the GPA has
[Page 1] not improved to at least 2.25, the student is dropped from the major. Students who experience extraordinary
[Page 1] circumstances may petition for one or more additional probationary quarters by contacting the academic
[Page 1] adviser.
