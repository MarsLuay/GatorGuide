Bachelor of Business Administration
Bachelor of Business Administration
Build the skills to lead in a changing world
Prepare to lead, innovate, and create impact in today&#8217;s dynamic business world. In the School of Business at the University of Washington Bothell, the Bachelor of Arts in Business Administration prepares you to think strategically, act ethically, and drive results in any industry. Through small classes, close mentorship, and connections to the Puget Sound business community, you will gain the confidence, knowledge, and experience to launch a meaningful career.
Why Choose Business at UW Bothell
Small classes, big impact. Learn in a collaborative environment where faculty know you by name.
Career-ready skills. Build expertise in leadership, analytics, strategy, and communication that employers seek.
Connections that matter. Access internships, networking, and mentorship across greater Seattle&#8217;s dynamic business community.
Support every step. Benefit from advising, faculty mentorship, and resources designed to help you succeed.
The Power of a UW Degree
Carry the distinction of a University of Washington degree, respected worldwide for excellence and opportunity.
What to Expect
Our curriculum and approach blends business fundamentals with practical, applied learning. You will:
Develop analytical and decision-making skills
Build leadership and innovation capacity
Develop a strategic mindset and global business perspective
Gain tangible skills through projects, internships, and case studies
Strengthen your ability to communicate and collaborate
Career Outcomes
Graduates are prepared for diverse opportunities in private, public, and nonprofit sectors. Roles include:
Technology manager, data analyst, or digital transformation specialist
Accountant, auditor, or financial analyst
Marketing manager, brand strategist, or sales lead
Business consultant, project manager, or entrepreneur
Many graduates also continue into advanced study, including the UW Bothell MBA.

Admissions Snapshot
Applicants are evaluated on GPA, prerequisite performance, and a personal statement.
Completion of prerequisite coursework is required before starting.
Transfer students begin at the junior level.
First-year students may directly enter this program .
