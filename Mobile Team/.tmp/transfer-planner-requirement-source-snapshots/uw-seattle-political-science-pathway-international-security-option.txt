[Page 1] General Information
[Page 1] The study of international security focuses on the relationship between politics and security and examines the causes of war and the
[Page 1] use and control of force: threats, promises, and the tactics, techniques, and ethics of violence. It teaches the theories and the historical
[Page 1] context for understanding contemporary security issues.
[Page 1] The option is recommended for students interested in an in-depth study of international security. This is an optional program of study
[Page 1] and not an official college major or minor. Students who elect this option must satisfy both the 50 credits required for the Political
[Page 1] Science major and the 45 credits for the option. Up to 25 credits may overlap between the Political Science major and the option. No
[Page 1] more than 20 credits may be used to satisfy both the option and the requirements for any other major. Non-Pol S courses count only
[Page 1] toward requirements for the option, not for the Political Science major. Courses for the option must have a grade of 2.0 or higher.
[Page 1] If you complete the 45-credit international security option, you will receive the notation Political Science: International Security
[Page 1] Option on your transcript after you graduate.
[Page 1] Declare the option when you have completed at least 6 courses or when you apply to graduate . The Political Science Advising
[Page 1] Office is located in 215 Smith Hall. If you have questions, call 543-1824 or email us at polsadvc@uw.edu.
