[Page 2] The Writing Center is open beginning the second week of each quarter and is located in Gowen 111.
[Page 2] Political Science Computer Lab : The Computer Lab is located in Smith 220. Lab hours are 9am to 4pm during each
[Page 2] academic quarter (reduced hours during final exam week and summer quarter). The lab is closed during quarter
[Page 2] breaks and when reserved for classes or events.
[Page 2] Internships : https://www.polisci.washington.edu/internships
[Page 2] The Political Science website has a page devoted to internships, including how to earn academic credit, internship
[Page 2] postings, and internship search tools. Also see listings in our Advising News.
[Page 2] The Political Science Department has longstanding relationships with two internship programs:
[Page 2] The Washington State Legislative Internship Program: http://www.leg.wa.gov/internships/Pages/default.aspx
[Page 2] Winter Quarter. Students work in Olympia as staff for members of the Washington State House of
[Page 2] Representatives or Senate. Admission is competitive. Applications are due in early-October. Mark
[Page 2] Weitzenkamp, weitzen@uw.edu, is the liaison for this program.
[Page 2] The Washington Center Internship Program : twc.edu/programs/academic-internship-program
[Page 2] Autumn Semester, Spring Semester, Summer Quarter. This 15 credit Washington, D.C., program finds
[Page 2] placements and provides housing and additional services to students who want to intern in our nation’s capital
[Page 2] either virtually or on site. Meera Roy, meroy@uw.edu, is the liaison for this program.

[Page 2] S 499 Independent Study or with affiliated centers. See the website for information about research opportunities
[Page 2] and enrolling in Pol S 499.
