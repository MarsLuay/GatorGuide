Major Requirements
SAFS majors are required to complete both the College of Environment&#8217;s General Education requirements and the School&#8217;s degree requirements listed below. These requirements lead to a Bachelor of Science degree, with a major in Aquatic Conservation and Ecology (ACE), or a major in Aquatic and Fishery Sciences (AFS).
NOTE: The AFS major is closed to new students. Information about the AFS major will remain on this site for reference for currently declared students.
College of the Environment General Education requirements
English Composition (C)
5 credits. Minimum 2.0 grade required
Reasoning (RSN)
10 credits. Calculus and Statistics, taken as part of the AFS degree requirements will fulfill this requirement.
Additional writing courses (W)
10 credits. Courses taken as part of core AFS degree requirements will fulfill RSN and W requirements.
Diversity (DIV)
5 credits
Natural Sciences (NSc)
20 credits. 10 credits of NSc must be outside the major (i.e., not a major prefix and no overlap with major). This is a good opportunity to take courses in Oceanography, Forestry, Biology, Atmospheric Sciences, Earth and Space Sciences, etc.
Social Sciences (SSc)
20 credits. 10 credits of SSc must be outside the major (i.e., not a major prefix and no overlap with major). AIS courses are recommended.
Arts and Humanities (A&H)
10 credits. CLAS 205 and COM 220 are recommended.
Additional Areas of Inquiry
10 credits
Total for General Education
= 88 credits
Please note that the College of the Environment does not have a foreign language requirement

Degree Courses by Quarter (subject to change w/o notice)

Basic Science Courses
Calculus and Statistics
(15 credits)
2 quarters of calculus, 1 quarter of probability and statistics
Q SCI 291, 292 or MATH 124, 125 (calculus)
Q SCI 381 or STAT 311 (statistics)
Chemistry
(10–15 credits)
1­–2 quarters of general chemistry, 1 quarter of organic chemistry
CHEM 120 or both CHEM 142, 152 (general chemistry)
CHEM 220, OCEAN 295, CHEM 223, or CHEM 237 (organic chemistry)
Majors are encouraged to take CHEM classes as soon as possible, as two quarters of chemistry are required to take BIOL 200 (see below)
Biology
(15 credits)
BIOL 180, 200
BIOL 220 or FISH 270
Majors are encouraged to complete the BIOL series as soon as possible, as many upper-level FISH Core and Elective courses have BIOL pre-requisites
Introductory Courses
Life in Water
(3–5 credits)
One of the following:
FISH 200 (3 or 5) Freshwater Ecology & Conservation
FISH 250 (5) Marine Biology
People and the Environment
(5 credits)
One of the following (or other, approved by petition )
ANTH 210 (5) Intro to Environmental Anthropology
ENVIR 235 (5) Intro to Environmental Economics
FISH 230 (5) Economics of Fisheries & Oceans
Connecting to Career Pathways
(1–2 credits)
One of the following:
FISH 300 (2) Professional Development in Aquatic Sciences
MARBIO 301 (1) Current Topics in Marine Biology
Programming & Data Science
(4 credits)
One of the following:
CSE 160 (4) Data Programming
Q SCI 256 (4) Intro to Data Science Methods for Environmental Sciences
Core Knowledge & Skills Courses
ACE Core
(15 credits) All of these courses will count towards the UW Additional Writing (W) requirement.
FISH 312 (5; prereq BIOL 220/FISH 270) Fisheries Ecology
FISH 323 (5) Conservation & Management of Aquatic Resources
One of the following:
FISH 340 (5; prereq BIOL 200) Genetics & Molecular Ecology
FISH 370 (5; prereq BIOL 220/FISH 270) Marine Evolutionary Biology
Communicating Science
(3 credits)
One of the following:
FISH 290 (3) Scientific Writing & Communication
MARBIO 305 (3) Scientific Writing in Marine Biology
Data Analysis & Modeling
(5 credits)
One of the following:
FISH 454 (5; prereqs Q SCI 292 or MATH 125, Q SCI 381 or STAT 311) Ecological Modeling
Q SCI 483 (5; prereq Q SCI 482) Statistical Inference in Applied Research II
Advanced Topics Courses
Students must complete at least 20 credits total of approved elective courses with the following distribution requirements:
At least 5 credits in the Aquatic Ecology category
At least 5 credits in the Conservation & Resource Management Issues category
At least 5 credits with the Quant/Data Science designation (Q) ; this may overlap with the above requirements
Aquatic Ecology
(min. 5 credits)
Options include:
FISH 406 (5; prereq BIOL 180) Parasite Ecology
FISH 437 (4) Fisheries Oceanography
FISH 447 (5; prereq BIOL 180, ESRM 201, or FISH 200) Watershed Ecology & Management
FISH 450 (4) Salmonid Behavior & Life History
FISH 464 (5; prereq BIOL 180) Arctic Marine Vertebrate Ecology
FISH 470 (5; prereqs Q SCI 381, FISH 250, and FISH 290 or MARBIO 305) Evolutionary Ecology of Marine Mammals
FISH 473 (3; prereq BIOL 180) Limnology
FISH 474 (2; pre/coreq: FISH 473) Limnology Lab
Aquatic Conservation & Resource Management Issues
(min. 5 credits)
Options include:
FHL 403 (5; prereq FISH 310, 312, 323, or 370) Novel Marine Ecosystems
FISH 423 (4; prereq BIOL 180) Aquatic Invasion Ecology
FISH 425 (5; prereq Q SCI 381 or STAT 311) Sustainable Aquaculture
FISH 428 (5; prereq FISH 312) Stream & Watershed Restoration
FISH 429 (3; prereq FISH 312 or 323) Coastal Restoration Ecology
FISH 431 (5; prereq CSE 160 or Q SCI 256) Modeling of Complex Systems (Q)
FISH 444 (5; prereq FISH 340 or 370) Conservation Genetics
FISH 458 (5; recommend FISH 454) Quantitative Conservation & Management (Q)
FISH 478 (3) Topics in Sustainable Fisheries
Practicum Option
(max. 7 credits) This is optional, but recommended for students who intend to pursue advanced degrees.
Students can complete the SAFS Capstone (review the Capstone Page for more information):
FISH 493 (1; prereq FISH 290) Capstone 1: Proposal
FISH 494 (3) Capstone 2: Research
FISH 495 (3) Capstone 3: Research Synthesis
Or students can complete independent study credits through either or a combination of the following:
FISH 498 (1-15) Internship/Experiential Learning
FISH 499 (1-15) Undergraduate Research
Practicum coursework may count toward the Quant/Data Science requirement by petition .
AFS Departmental Degree requirements
The AFS major is closed to new students. Students who declared AFS before Autumn 2025 will be able to continue in the major until they graduate.

Upper Level FISH Courses
Science Writing
(3 credits)
FISH 290 (3) Scientific Writing & Communication
Natural History
(10 credits)
FISH 310 (5) Biology of Shellfish
FISH 311 (5) Biology of Fishes
It is recommended that students have completed at least 10 credits of the BIOL series prior to taking these courses
AFS Core
(15 credits) All of these courses will count towards the UW Additional Writing (W) requirement.
Take three of the following courses:
FISH 312 (5; prereq BIOL 220/FISH 270) Fisheries Ecology
FISH 323 (5) Conservation & Management of Aquatic Resources
FISH 324 (5; prereq BIOL 220/FISH 270) Aquatic Animal Physiology & Reproduction
FISH 340 (5; prereq BIOL 200) Genetics & Molecular Ecology
400-level FISH Electives
(min. 4 courses and 16 credits)
Choose four 400-level FISH classes from the list on the Courses page.
Restrictions:
Excludes FISH 453, 477, 496, and all capstone courses (FISH 493, 494, 495).
FISH 497 Special Topics may not automatically apply to the 400-level requirement—see individual offerings.
A maximum of 3 credits total from FISH 479, 498, 499, and FISH/FHL 492 may be applied.
A maximum of 5 credits from each of FISH 489 and 491 may be applied.
Capstone Research
(7 credits) Review the Capstone page for more information.
FISH 493 (1; prereq FISH 290) Capstone 1: Proposal
FISH 494 (3) Capstone 2: Research
FISH 495 (3) Capstone 3: Research Synthesis
