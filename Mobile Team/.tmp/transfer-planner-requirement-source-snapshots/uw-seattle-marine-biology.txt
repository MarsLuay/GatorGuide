[Page 5] INTEGRATIVE FIELD EXPERIENCE (6 credits)
[Page 5] Students select from a list of eligible courses which give hands- on experience with changes in the marine environment at a variety of
[Page 5] scales (over time and space) and systems (physical and biological). These advanced courses build on the foundational knowl edge
[Page 5] developed across the three core areas of the major (biodiversity, ecology and ecosystems and organismal processes) and develo p skills in
[Page 5] fieldwork techniques, data analysis and science communication.
[Page 5] • Most eligible courses are offered at the UW’s marine field station Friday Harbor Labs (FHL).
[Page 5] • Courses are grouped by academic programs, which vary depending on quarter
[Page 5] • Time spent away from campus varies by program, ranging from a minimum of three weeks to a full academic quarter.
[Page 5] • Academic programs can also include additional unique courses and credits which can be applied to Marine Biology Major
[Page 5] requirements.
[Page 5] FHL Application
[Page 5] All Integrative Field Experience courses require a Friday Harbor Labs application.
[Page 5] • Starting in Winter 2026, Marine Biology majors will still be able to apply for summer FHL course offerings through the general FHL
[Page 5] application. However, Marine Biology majors will no longer be prioritized over other applicants.
[Page 5] • Marine Biology majors can receive preferential admissions to spring, early fall and autumn academic programs based on
[Page 5] progress in major requirements as well as time to graduate. Marine Biology majors can apply to summer quarter academic
[Page 5] programs, but are not granted preferential admissions due to increased competition for limited seats. Preferential admission is
[Page 5] granted for Marine Biology majors based on the following criteria:
[Page 5] o Time to graduate
[Page 5] o Completion of, or progress towards the following Marine Biology Major requirements:
[Page 5]  Introductory Marine Science: OCEAN 210
[Page 5]  Physiology: FISH/OCEAN/MARBIO 270 or BIOL 220
[Page 5]  Communications: MARBIO 305 or FHL 333
[Page 5]  Statistics: Q SCI 381 or STAT 311
[Page 5]  Core: at least one of FISH 323, OCEAN 330 or MARBIO/FISH/OCEAN 370
[Page 5]  Other Marine Biology Major electives
[Page 5] IFE Options
[Page 5] Spring
[Page 5] Zoology Botany (Zoo-Bot) Program (16–17 cr): Full-term spring (approximately 10 weeks)
[Page 5] Students enroll in three courses (FHL 430: Marine Zoology, FHL 440, Marine Botany and FHL 470: Research in Marine Biology) with an
[Page 5] optional 1 credit seminar. Students earn major elective credits in addition to the IFE requirement.
[Page 5] Spring Marine Studies (16-17 cr): Full-term Spring quarter (approximately 10 weeks)
[Page 5] Students enroll in three courses + an optional 1 credit seminar (FHL 490). Students earn 10 major elective credits in addition to the
[Page 5] integrative field experience requirement.
[Page 5] Summer – Marine Biology majors can apply for summer FHL course offerings through the general FHL application. However, Marine
[Page 5] Biology majors will no longer be prioritized over other applicants.
[Page 5] FHL 468: Advanced Topics in Ecology & Biomechanics (9 cr): Half-term summer (approximately 5 weeks)
[Page 5] Students enroll in a single, nine-credit course. Topics vary by section and year, and eligible courses are approved annually. Approved courses
[Page 5] complete the six-credit integrative field experience requirement with the remaining three credits applied to ‘other marine biology elective’.
[Page 5] FHL 492: Ecology & Conservation of Marine Birds & Mammals (9 cr): Half-term summer (approximately 5 weeks) Students enroll in a single,
[Page 5] nine-credit course. Offered every other summer in “odd years.” Students earn 3 credits of major elective in addition to the IFE requirement.
[Page 5] Early Fall
[Page 5] MARBIO 488: Marine Biology in the Field (6 cr) Three weeks prior to the start of fall quarter (exact dates vary by year) . Standard, campus-based
[Page 5] course offered annually in the fall with a mandatory field component covered in the three weeks prior to the start of fall quarter (exact dates
[Page 5] vary by year) at Friday Harbor Labs. The FHL field component consists of short research training modules focused on intertidal ecology, trophic
[Page 5] ecology, and oceanography followed by independent research projects conducted in small groups. The on-campus portion focuses on
[Page 5] analysis, presentation, and communication of research project results.
[Page 5] Fall
[Page 5] Autumn Marine Studies (16cr): Full-term fall quarter (approximately 10 weeks)
[Page 5] Students enroll in three courses + an optional 1 credit seminar. Students earn 10 major elective credits in addition to the integrative
[Page 5] field experience requirement.
[Page 5] Research Apprenticeship: FHL Apprenticeship (15 cr): Full-term fall quarter (approximately 10 weeks)
[Page 5] Students engage in advanced undergraduate research in a small cohort of students. Topics vary by section and year, and eligible
[Page 5] apprenticeships are approved on a section basis. Approved apprenticeships complete the six-credit IFE requirement with six additional
[Page 5] credits applied to ‘other marine biology elective’ and the remaining three credits applied to “non major elective.”
